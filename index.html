zoea

Children’s education for ocean and small animal protection

the problem

Children and families have little to no access to educational content about marine life and the impact of aquaculture.

Shrimp farming in Brazil involves cruel practices such as live transport and eyestalk ablation.

Society shows low levels of empathy towards small and aquatic animals.

The solution

Creation and free distribution of anti-speciesist educational materials focused on shrimp

Short animation: Zoe, from the mangroves to the sea

Tactile children’s books

Online interactive games

Designed for public/private schools and cultural spaces.

Target audience

Children aged 4 to 8 (early literacy phase)

Educators, schools, cultural and community centers

Families seeking meaningful and values-based education

Approach

First initiative in Brazil focused on empathy with aquatic animals through early childhood education

Inclusive, sensory-based approach (tactile books)

Scalable adaptable to other species and contexts

Expected impact

Increased empathy toward small animals

Critical thinking about food systems and habits from an early age

Greater awareness of marine ecosystems and their value

Sustentability model

Initial funding from Kickstarting For Good and cultural grants

Applications to public and private funding calls

Visibility and support from short film festivals and awards

Donations and strategic partnerships with schools and NGOs
